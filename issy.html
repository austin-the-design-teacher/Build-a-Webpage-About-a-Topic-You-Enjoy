Equine Biomechanics

Equine biomechanics can be defined as the scientific study of equines, focusing on anatomical struggles to promote balance and fluidity of motion; used for the diagnoises of muscular injuries (such as lameness and laminitis) and for higher performance at the competetove level.

General Information

  • Biomechanics are used in every discipline
  • Biomechanics focus on:
    • working on springs in legs
    • hind end strength
    • center of gravity
    • Rider impact on posture
    • hoof flexibility
    • gait analysis
    • risk of damaging tendons during movement
  • Ex. Currently with my horse, we are working on building her hind end strength for a more powerful canter up to jumps and power during courses
